Willow Creek Wildlife Park News

Celebrating 50 Years of Wildlife Conservation

Join us May 15th in celebrating the 50th Anniversary of Willow Creek Wildlife Park! This special event will feature a commemorative ceremony, a walk through our park's history, and a special anniversary edition of our wildlife talks. We are also unveiling a new educational exhibit highlighting the milestones we’ve reached in animal conservation over the past five decades.

Over the years, Willow Creek Wildlife Park has grown from a small sanctuary for local wildlife to one of the leading wildlife conservation centers in the region. We've been instrumental in rescuing endangered species and providing sanctuary for animals in need. This milestone represents the hard work and dedication of our staff, volunteers, and the community.

As part of the celebrations, we're unveiling a new interactive timeline of our 50-year journey, showcasing the success stories of conservation efforts. The timeline will be featured prominently in the park's newly renovated visitor center, and will also be available online for everyone to explore.

We are also hosting a special wildlife talk at 2:00 PM, where our lead conservation biologist will share insights into how Willow Creek has contributed to the preservation of endangered species and how we continue to push the boundaries of animal welfare and environmental sustainability.

Grand Opening of the Arctic Habitat

We are excited to announce the opening of our new Arctic Habitat on June 1st! The exhibit features stunning replicas of the Arctic tundra and is home to a variety of cold-climate species, including polar bears, arctic wolves, and snowy owls. Come meet our new residents and enjoy live animal feedings, zookeeper talks, and interactive exhibits for all ages.

Inside Our Endangered Species Breeding Program

Our Breeding Program for endangered species is helping save some of the world’s most vulnerable animals. In this exclusive behind-the-scenes event on June 12th, you’ll learn about the efforts we’re making to protect species such as the Amur leopard, the black-footed ferret, and the California condor. Hear from our experts and get an up-close look at our conservation work.
